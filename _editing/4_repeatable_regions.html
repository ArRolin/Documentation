--- title: Repeatable Regions ---

Repeatable Regions are useful anywhere you have repeating content. For example, staff profiles or a simple news feed. Users can add, move and delete the content inside a repeatable region.

To use Repeatable Regions, add class="repeatables" to an element in your HTML. The element's direct children then become repeatable regions. Note: All the direct children must all be the same type of element (i.e. div, p, li). To make the content inside the repeatable region updatable you can use editable regions.

Let's go through a quick example for a news section. First, add the HTML for a single news item and then wrap it in a div with a class of repeatables: 

<div class="repeatables">
  <div class="editable">
    <h2>10/01/13 - Exciting News Item!</h2>
    <p>Text for the news item.</p>
  </div>
</div>

Save this and switch to the Visual Editor. The news item now has a yellow icon at top left.

Click on the icon to clone the news item. The HTML will now look like this: 

<div class="repeatables">
  <div class="editable">
    <h2>10/01/13 - Exciting News Item!</h2>
    <p>Text for the news item.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="editable">
    <h2>10/01/13 - Exciting News Item!</h2>
    <p>Text for the news item.</p>
  </div>
</div>

Editable regions work within repeatable regions so users can continue to update content inline. Hovering over the news items now displays arrow icons for reordering and a trash can to delete a news item.

Repeatable regions work great in simple cases. For more complex situations, see our Jekyll section.
